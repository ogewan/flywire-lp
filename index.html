FlyWire is an online community open to all Drosophila neuroscientists, working towards a whole-brain wiring diagram of the adult fly. Using a female brain sectioned and imaged by electron microscopy by Zheng et al. (2018), we applied artificial intelligence to automatically reconstruct neurons in 3D within the images. The AI reconstructs large segments of both neurons and glia, often close to complete, which our users can then assemble into full cells and circuits, using FlyWire’s online proofreading tools. All cells are part of a single large dataset, openly shared within the community.

Once you apply, you’ll complete self-guided training and then receive full edit access. Working in FlyWire is similar to playing a video game, but the results are beautiful cells and circuits that will both advance your own research, and help to crowdsource the whole-brain connectome.
