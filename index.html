a human-AI collaboration to map the fly connectome

FlyWire is an online community open to all Drosophila neuroscientists, working towards a whole-brain wiring diagram of the adult fly. Using a female brain sectioned and imaged by electron microscopy by Zheng et al. (2018), we applied artificial intelligence to detect cells in the slices and reconstruct them in 3D. The AI finds large segments of neurons and glia, often close to complete, which our users then assemble into full cells and circuits, using FlyWire’s online proofreading tools. All cells are part of a single large dataset, openly shared within the community. Once you apply, you’ll take our self-guided training then receive edit access.

It’s like playing an addictive video game, but the results are beautiful cells and circuits that both advance your own research, and help to crowdsource the whole-brain connectome.
